Rules

Below is an example of a Hydrograph. In this graphic you can see that there are three magnitudes of flooding shown: 2-year, 25-year, and 100-year periods. The lines represent the various statistical values that are used to ensure the accuracy and precision of the forecast model. To simplify the decision-making process, focus on the blue line representing the average of all the predicted possibilities and the black line which represents the prediction based on a more detailed history. Remember the following as you play the game:

1) Select which mitigative measure you feel will best protect the community by minimizing loss of life and damage based the new hydrograph displayed each day.

2) You may use each mitigative action only once in the 10-day period preceding the target date.
